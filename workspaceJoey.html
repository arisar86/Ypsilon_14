The Workspace

Work boots clang and echo through the workspace of the mining station. The air is damp and heavy, a mix of sweat and oily machinery. You take a deep breath and look around. On the left wall cubies are lining up, probably stowing the gear of the workers. Just next to the cubies a cramped corridor leads to the crews quarters. To your right a massive generator sits in one corner by an industrial vent alongside a computer terminal slowly bleeping out lines of data. In the opposite corner, white sheet plastic and sterile lightning marks a semi-permanent Medbay. The floor in the middle of the room opens up into a yawning pit, with a mechanised drill and pump system penetrating down into the dark mine shaft. A rather spartan freight elevator carries miners to and from the mine entrance.
