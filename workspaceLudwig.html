The Workspace

Work boots clang and echo through the workspace of the mining station. The air is damp and heavy. Your hardware runs automatic tests on the air that fills your artificial lungs as you breath it in. Red numbers flashing on your Retina display inform you about the low oxygen quality in the room. You switch off the warning signal and look around. On the left wall cubies are lining up, probably stowing the gear of the workers. Just next to the cubies a cramped corridor leads to the crews quarters. To your right a massive generator sits in one corner by an industrial vent alongside a computer terminal slowly bleeping out lines of data. In the opposite corner, white sheet plastic and sterile lightning marks a semi-permanent Medbay. The floor in the middle of the room opens up into a yawning pit, with a mechanised drill and pump system penetrating down into the dark mine shaft. A rather spartan freight elevator carries miners to and from the mine entrance.
